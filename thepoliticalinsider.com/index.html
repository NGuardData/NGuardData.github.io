thepoliticalinsider.com  (WEB - en-US)

A political news and commentary website owned by an advertising company that works with conservative political action committees. The site regularly publishes false and misleading stories and does not disclose its funding sources, editors, or content creators.

Proceed with caution: This website severely violates basic standards of credibility and transparency.

Ownership and Financing

The Political Insider LLC is a Reston, Virginia-based company owned by Aurora Media Inc. According to its website, Aurora Media “operates closely with Intermarkets,” an advertising company that solicits advertisements from conservative political action committees and publishes them largely on conservative sites including the Drudge Report and CNSNews.com.

According to Federal Election Commission records, in 2015 and 2016, Intermarkets received over $88,000 in payments from the Republican National Committee for advertising and list acquisition. Since 2007, Intermarkets has received over $1,322,789 from mainly conservative political groups for services including online ads, email list purchases, and fundraising phone calls.

Aurora Media’s founder and chief executive officer is Kevin P. Lucido, who also founded Intermarkets. Steven Castleton, CEO of Dionysus Consulting, which previously owned ThePoliticalInsider.com, told NewsGuard that he sold the site to Intermarkets in July 2013.

Content

ThePoliticalInsider.com says it offers an insider’s perspective on politics, with reporters who have “experience working on Capitol Hill, on campaigns, and in conservative activism, reporting, and media.” However, the staff biographies listed on the About page identify most of the site’s six staff members as conservative bloggers. One of its contributors is Wayne Dupree, the host of a conservative radio show who has promoted the conspiracy theory that the Sandy Hook school massacre was staged.

The site divides its content into Current, Politics, Culture, and Popular pages. Despite these apparent distinctions, almost all original Political Insider content is labeled as “News Commentary.” Stories are based on tweets, videos, or reporting from other news sources, accompanied by commentary from Political Insider writers. The site also publishes stories from the Daily Caller News Foundation, the nonprofit arm of the Daily Caller, a website founded by conservative news commentator Tucker Carlson.

Credibility

A NewsGuard review of ThePoliticalInsider.com has found multiple instances of false reporting, or distortion of facts to advance a pro-Republican and anti-Democrat perspective, over the past two years. The stories were not updated or corrected. The false or misleading reports include:

**A July 2018 article that contained a claim that President Obama had separated 89,000 children from their parents at the U.S. border, compared with 1,900 children under the Trump administration. The Obama administration did not practice a policy that separated families, and a 2016 Associated Press story reported that more than 89,000 migrant children were placed with sponsors between 2013 and 2016, but that number included children who arrived at the U.S. border unaccompanied.

**A December 2016 report titled “BREAKING: Sheriff Joe Drops MASSIVE Bombshell About Obama’s Birth Certificate! (VIDEO),” that falsely reported that Obama’s birth certificate had been forged. Hawaii state officials have denied the long-running conspiracy theory multiple times.  

**A November 2016 story that posted an artist’s rendering of a lookalike posing as former president Bill Clinton and falsely said the photo showed him in bed with an unidentified woman. The Political Insider later took the article down, without publishing a correction.

**A June 2018 article titled “Trump Is The Most Popular Republican President Since WWII,” that reported on a Gallup poll measuring presidential approval ratings after 500 days in office. The data actually reported that Trump’s approval rating fell behind that of George W. Bush, which was accurately reported within the story, but not in the headline. The data also only tracked approval ratings among members of the president’s own party.

NewsGuard was unable to local corrections on the site.

The site did not respond to NewsGuard’s questions in five emails and two telephone calls regarding its publication of false, misleading, and uncorrected reports.

Fact-checking sites Politifact, Snopes, and FactCheck.org have cited four Political Insider stories as containing false content. The Political Insider is also listed on Politifact’s Fake News Almanac, a list of every website where Politifact has found “deliberately false or fake stories.”

Transparency

The Political Insider’s Contact page has a form for users to reach the site, and the About page provides an email address for readers to suggest corrections. It also lists first names and short biographies for the site’s contributors, in addition to some social media handles. Dupree and five others are identified by first name only, with two authors providing their Twitter handles. No identifying information is provided for most others. The biographies do not indicate individual contributors’ roles, and some articles are attributed to an unnamed “guest author.”

The site does not disclose its relationship with Aurora Media or Intermarkets, nor does it reveal the political nature of its sources of funding.

NewsGuard’s questions in five emails and two telephone calls regarding the site’s disclosures about its owners, editors, and content creators received no response.

Advertisements and sponsored content are distinguished from editorial content.

History

When The Political Insider was founded in 2006, according to Aurora Media, it was “an opt-in email list for political donors and activists.” The site’s former owner, Dionysus Consulting, is a privately owned Internet consulting company that has provided online fundraising for Republican political campaigns. After a July 2013 webpage sale to Intermarkets, The Political Insider was relaunched in 2014 by Aurora Media Inc. as “a social media and digital news publication,” according to a press release.
