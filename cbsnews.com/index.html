Ownership and Financing

CBS News is owned by the publicly traded CBS Corporation and is part of the CBS Television Network. CBSNews.com is operated by CBS Interactive, which also runs the food site Chowhound, the technology site CNET, and several sports, entertainment, and gaming websites. CBS Interactive’s revenue primarily comes from advertising and sponsorships.

Content

CBSNews.com publishes national and international news, opinion, and in-depth investigations in articles, videos, and podcasts.

CBSN, the site’s live, 24/7 digital streaming service, has a separate page on the site, publishing breaking news and its own content such as “CBSN: On Assignment.” CBSN’s live reports are frequently aired on the homepage. It also simulcasts CBS News special reports and rebroadcasts other network programs.

The network’s mainstay news programs — “CBS Evening News,” “CBS This Morning,” “48 Hours,” “60 Minutes,” “CBS Sunday Morning,” and “Face the Nation” — have dedicated pages. CBSNews.com links to the sites of 23 local television stations owned and operated by CBS.

CBS content is written and produced by the CBS News staff, which includes many well-known journalists, such as “60 Minutes” correspondents Steve Kroft and Lesley Stahl and morning show anchors Gayle King, John Dickerson, and Norah O’Donnell.

The website divides content into national and international news sections, and has separate pages for politics, entertainment, sports, health, crime, and MoneyWatch.

Credibility

Reporting on CBSNews.com is objective and fact-based, citing expert sources, firsthand accounts from CBS News reporters, surveys, and primary documents. Commentary, which generally appears in the site’s politics section, is clearly labeled. The site does not have a stated corrections policy, but regularly corrects errors, attaching italicized notes to articles that explain the error and the change.

CBS News programs consistently win awards. “60 Minutes” has won 138 Emmy Awards since it launched in 1968, the most of any primetime broadcast. The network won nine Emmys in 2017, and seven Edward R. Murrow Awards in 2018, including one for general excellence.

CBS received awards for a three-part series in 2017 by “60 Minutes” and The Washington Post that detailed how the pharmaceutical industry blocked U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration efforts to combat the country’s opioid crisis. The reporting prompted calls for legislation and the withdrawal of U.S. President Donald Trump’s drug czar nominee. The reporting was honored with the Peabody Award, the Hillman Prize for Broadcast Journalism, and a Murrow award.

The network has also weathered high-profile controversies. CBS retracted a “60 Minutes” story by “CBS Evening News” anchor Dan Rather in 2004 that alleged then-President George W. Bush was given preferential treatment when he served in the Texas Air National Guard during the Vietnam War era. CBS fired the segment’s producer and demanded the resignations of three network executives. Rather stepped down as anchor after 24 years.

CBS retracted another “60 Minutes” story, about the 2012 attack in Benghazi, Libya, that killed four American diplomats. The segment, reported by correspondent Lara Logan, focused on an eyewitness account by Dylan Davies, a former British security contractor. Subsequent reports by The Washington Post and The New York Times found that Davies’ account differed from what he had told his employer and the FBI. Logan apologized on air and took a leave of absence along with the segment’s producer.

In 1982, General William C. Westmoreland, who headed U.S. forces during the Vietnam War, sued the network for libel over a documentary CBS News produced on the Vietnam War. Westmoreland withdrew the $120 million lawsuit after 18 weeks of testimony.

Transparency

Stories published on CBSNews.com are sometimes credited to reporters, and sometimes credited only to CBS News. There is biographical information for some journalists, but users cannot easily access this information from the homepage. Because content is inconsistently credited, and contact and biographical information is not easily accessible, NewsGuard has determined that CBSNews.com does not adequately provide information about content creators. CBS declined to comment when asked about the lack of disclosure.

The site posts extensive biographies for CBS top executives on CBS’ About page. Each of the network’s shows includes a staff list.

Users can contact CBS News through a general form and post comments on the site’s content. Most CBS News shows, aside from “CBS This Morning,” provide a physical address, phone number, and general email.

CBS launched a blog called “Public Eye,” an ombudsman-like service, after an internal investigation concerning the 2004 “60 Minutes” story about President Bush’s National Guard service. The blog was discontinued after three years amid staff cutbacks.

An About page on CBSNews.com links to the website of its owner, CBS Corporation.

Advertising and sponsored content are distinguishable from editorial content.

History

CBS first signed onto the air as a radio network in 1928. It was established by William S. Paley, who bought 16 independent radio stations and called them the Columbia Broadcasting System. Edward R. Murrow was a famed radio broadcaster for CBS during World War II. CBS aired its first television broadcast in 1941.

In 1963, CBS aired the first 30-minute daily news broadcast on “CBS Evening News.” Walter Cronkite, anchor of the broadcast from 1962-1981 and one of the most prominent figures in television news, was known as “the most trusted man in America,” a phrase CBS based on a 1972 Oliver Quayle poll. His famous nightly sign-off from the broadcast was, “And that’s the way it is.”

Beginning in 2017, CBS hired an outside law firm to investigate claims by at least 27 women, including 14 CBS News employees, who said they were sexually harassed or assaulted by Charlie Rose, who was fired in November as a “CBS This Morning” anchor and “60 Minutes” contributor. The network dismissed Rose after The Washington Post detailed accounts in November 2017 by eight women. At least three women have sued CBS, which said it had not been aware of the conduct. The Post reported in May 2018 that CBS managers were warned three times about his behavior over 30 years.

"60 Minutes" executive producer Jeff Fager was ousted by CBS in September 2018 amid allegations of sexual harassment. His removal, which was prompted by a threatening text message he sent to a CBS News correspondent who was reporting on the allegations, occurred three days after CBS Corporation Chairman and CEO Leslie Moonves stepped down, also amid sexual misconduct charges from multiple women.

In January 2019, the network announced that David Rhodes would step down as president of the news division and be replaced by veteran producer Susan Zirinsky, who was the inspiration for Holly Hunter’s character in the 1987 film “Broadcast News.”

The CBSNews.com domain name was registered in 1994. CBSNews.com went through a major expansion in 2005, emphasizing 24-hour digital coverage.
