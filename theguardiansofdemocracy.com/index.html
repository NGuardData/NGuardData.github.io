theguardiansofdemocracy.com  (WEB - en-US)

An anonymously operated political news site that publishes stories criticizing Republicans and the Trump administration.

Proceed with caution: This website severely violates basic standards of credibility and transparency.

Ownership

NewsGuard could not confirm who owns the site, although it found several connections between the site and an online publisher called Miami Media Group. The group posted a content writing internship for The Guardians of Democracy on an online job board called Internships.com. It described itself as “an umbrella company with multiple media platforms, including The Guardians of Democracy, which is a leading political newsletter in the United States.”

The Guardians of Democracy’s domain was registered by Adrian Garcia, who describes himself on LinkedIn as CEO and founder of The Gaily Grind, an online LGBTQ+ news magazine that is owned by Miami Media Group.

TheGuardiansofDemocracy.com runs advertisements.

Content

The Guardians of Democracy states on its About page that it is “a transpartisan organization of citizen journalists and grassroots activists tasked with delivering the truth and defending the fundamental tenets of democracy, while also working to create new dimensions of democracy...” The section also quotes an extensive excerpt from Barack Obama’s farewell address as president.

The site’s ’s coverage mostly focuses on the perceived misdeeds of Republicans. Typical headlines include “Trump Proudly Declares He Is A ‘Nationalist’: ‘We’re Not Supposed To Use That Word,’” “McConnell Confronted At Restaurant By Angry Constituents Over His Plan To Cut Social Security, Medicare,” and “Trump Supporters Chant ‘Lock Her Up’ At Rally Just Hours After Bomb Mailed To Clinton Home.”

The articles typically rewrite stories that have been reported by other, credible news outlets, such as The Washington Post, The Hill, Reuters, and CNN. It divides content into sections that include Democracy, Climate, Equality, Justice, Freedom, and Economy.

Content is also frequently republished from TheConversation.com, a news website that allows other outlets to publish its content for free.

Credibility

Articles on TheGuardiansOfDemocracy.com consistently credit and link to their sources, and the site embeds video and social media material when relevant. Photos, however, do not usually include attributions. Headlines represent their stories’ content.

Although the site has not identified itself as politically liberal, it consistently publishes unflattering and critical stories about President Donald Trump and the Republican Party. Typical headlines include “Trump Suggested Spain Build A 3,000 Mile Wall Across The Sahara Desert To Solve Migration Crisis: Report,” “Ex-CIA Director Rips Trump’s False Attacks: Mueller’s Probe Is ‘Neither Illegal Nor A Witch Hunt,’” and “Trump, Under Investigation By Mueller, Says Hillary Colluded With Russia As Crowd Chants ‘Lock Her Up.’” 

Because much of the site’s content is critical of conservatives and the Trump administration, although The Guardians of Democracy does not clearly disclose a liberal perspective,  NewsGuard has determined that TheGuardiansOfDemocracy.com does not handle the difference between news and opinion responsibly.

The site has not posted a corrections policy, and NewsGuard did not find examples of published corrections. Three Newsguard emails -- one sent through the site’s Contact Us form and two to Garcia -- asking about the site’s corrections procedures and its disclosure of a political perspective did not receive a response.

Transparency

Articles on TheGuardiansOfDemocracy.com do not usually name their authors. Most stories are published under the byline, “Guardians of Democracy Staff.”

The Guardians of Democracy website does not disclose its ownership or editorial leaders. The site provides a Contact Us page allowing users to message The Guardians of Democracy.

Three Newsguard emails -- one sent through the site’s Contact Us form and two to Garcia -- asking about the site’s disclosure of owners, editors, and staff did not receive a response.

Advertising on TheGuardiansOfDemocracy.com is distinguished from editorial content.

History

In January 2017, TheGuardiansOfDemocracy.com domain and its associated Facebook page were registered. Its sister site, The Gaily Grind, was founded in January 2013.

Correction: An earlier version of this Nutrition Label misstated the number of emails that NewsGuard sent inquiring about The Guardians of Democracy’s editorial practices. NewsGuard sent three emails.
