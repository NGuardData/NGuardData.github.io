Ownership and Financing

Discover is owned by Kalmbach Publishing Co., a private company based in Waukesha, Wisconsin. The company publishes other special interest magazines, including Astronomy, Drone360, and Classic Trains. Kalmbach also owns the gaming company Rather Dashing Games.

Discover sells digital and print subscriptions. The site runs advertisements and sponsored content. It also links to an online shop offering science books, games and other products. The magazine is published in print 10 times a year.

Content

DiscoverMagazine.com’s tagline is "science for the curious." The site covers science topics and news for a general audience. Articles explore complex topics and scientific developments, including environmental issues and medical breakthroughs. It posts longform articles from its print edition and offers more than two dozen science and tech blogs covering a wide range of topics. D-brief, for example, provides daily articles about the latest science and tech news. Out There is a space and physics blog, while Lovesick Cyborg focuses on technology. Dead Things covers topics such as dinosaurs and lost civilizations.

Typical stories on the site include the uncertain future of driverless cars and the end of NASA's Dawn mission to Vesta and Ceres, two of the largest objects in the asteroid belt. The website also writes about advances in medicine, such as the use of Psilocybin mushrooms to help treat a range of mental disorders and the dangers of synthetic marijuana.

Credibility

Most articles feature multiple interviews with researchers, scholars, and other other experts about complex scientific issues. Authors frequently cite research published by scientific journals, such as The American Association for the Advancement of Science’s Science Robotics and the PNAS journal published by the National Academy of Sciences.

Headlines provide an accurate description of the stories, sometimes from a humorous angle, such as 'Do farts carry germs? Well, it depends on whether you are wearing pants" and “First-Known Interstellar Object Looks...Pretty Weird.” The site does not publish opinion content.

The magazine's corrections policy is not available online, but corrections are generally posted at the bottom of stories.  

Discover Magazine won a 2015 Science in Society Award in the Science Reporting category for an article about the power of the placebo effect.

Transparency

The magazine discloses that it is a property of Kalmbach Publishing at the bottom of the website and on its Staff page. Discover editors, bloggers and senior staff are listed on the site.

The website does not include any biographical or contact information for its content creators. According to its editorial FAQ page, the site does "not give personal contact information for our authors and contributors… As many of our authors are freelancers, we are not necessarily aware of their schedules or availability, and therefore cannot guarantee a response on their behalf.”

Discover did not respond to two emails seeking comment on its limited disclosure about its content creators.

Ads and sponsored links are distinguishable from editorial content.

History

Time Inc. launched Discover magazine in 1980. It was since sold three times and was acquired by Kalmbach Publishing from New York-based Discover Media in 2010. In 2013, Kalmbach moved Discover’s staff from New York to its headquarters in Waukesha, Wisconsin.
