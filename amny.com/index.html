Ownership and Financing

amNewYork is owned by Newsday Media Group, named after the Long Island-based tabloid newspaper Newsday. Patrick Dolan, a senior network adviser for News 12 Networks, which airs around Greater New York City, is effectively the sole owner of the company.

The Dolan family, including Patrick, are the controlling owners of the Madison Square Garden company, as well as the company’s sports teams, including the NBA’s New York Knicks and the NHL’s New York Rangers -- teams that amny.com covers.

amNY.com runs display advertisements.

Content

amNewYork describes its audience as “a younger, professional and more urban demographic than traditional newspapers.” Its journalists cover New York City government, sports, transportation, business, law enforcement, breaking news, and lifestyle topics.

A section titled Secrets of New York bills itself as the “ultimate insider's guide to the best-kept secrets of NYC's must-see places and buzzed-about people.” Articles provide interesting tidbits about New York City and highlight things to do. Some headlines include: “Manhattan: Weird facts you should know about the NYC borough,” and “Secrets of Citi Field, home to the New York Mets.”

The site’s coverage is sometimes supplemented with stories written by Newsday reporters.

Credibility

amNewYork’s local coverage is usually produced by its staff of approximately 20 reporters and editors, according to the site’s staff directory. Reports on amNY.com typically cite credible, firsthand sources such as public officials and government documents. Some stories link to reporting from other outlets, such as the Staten Island Advance and The New York Times.

The site has a separate Opinion section for its editorials, columns, and editorial cartoons, and  headlines generally reflect articles’ content.

NewsGuard could not locate a description of a corrections policy on the site, but amNewYork publishes corrections. Notices are usually italicized and appear underneath updated articles.

Transparency

A Contact Us section found at the bottom of every page gives the names and email addresses for the publication’s staff and editorial leaders. A separate Staff & Bios page provides brief biographies for reporters. amNY.com articles are generally published with author names and provide timestamps showing when they were most recently updated.

The Contact Us section also states that the publication is “part of Newsday Media Group.”

AMny.com’s staff coverage of the Knicks and the New York Rangers do not disclose that the publication’s owner, Patrick Dolan is part owner of Madison Square Garden, which owns the teams. That omission has led NewsGuard to determine that the site does not meet adequate disclosure of ownership.

After initially speaking with NewsGuard by telephone and through email, Newsday Media Group communications manager Kim Como did not respond to to an email and phone message seeking comment about the lack of ownership disclosure.

Advertising is distinguished from the site’s editorial content.

History

amNewYork was founded in 2003.

In 2005, the newspaper’s editor-in-chief, Alex Storozynski, resigned following a plagiarism controversy about a story he wrote about the outing of the identity of “Deep Throat” in the Watergate scandal. Newsday said at the time that a story that Storozynski wrote used passages from a Washington Post article without attribution. Storozynski challenged that description, telling Poynter in an email that his story mentioned the Post three times, and called the attribution controversy a “smokescreen.”
