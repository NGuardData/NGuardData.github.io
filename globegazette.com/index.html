Ownership and Financing

The Globe Gazette is owned by Lee Enterprises, a publicly traded, Davenport, Iowa-based company that owns more than 40 daily newspapers, including The St. Louis Post-Dispatch. Lee Enterprises’ newspapers generate revenue from advertising and subscriptions to their print and digital editions.

Content

The website covers Mason City and surrounding communities in northern Iowa. Articles are organized into sections for News, Obituaries, Opinion, Sports, Entertainment, and Lifestyles.  The site also dedicates a section to the Up in Smoke BBQ Bash, an annual culinary competition hosted by the Globe Gazette. Typical stories include a theft at a Walmart, repairs to a bridge, high school sports, and a data breach at a medical center involving the health records and personal information of 1,900 people.

Some content is produced by the Globe Gazette editorial staff. Other articles come from other Lee-owned publications including the Sioux City Journal, and wire services including The Associated Press.

The site carries content from its partner weekly newspapers in the region, including the Britt News Tribune, the Forest City Summit, and the Mitchell County Press.

Credibility

Articles contain original reporting and headlines accurately reflect content of stories. Commentary is placed in a separate Opinion section and clearly labeled. The Globe Gazette regularly corrects errors in articles and discloses the correction in an editor’s note at the top or bottom of the story.  

Trans

A Contact page includes the newsroom’s street address and phone numbers for departments at the paper. A Staff page names each member of the editorial, business, and managerial staff, often including his or her email address and phone number. Most articles name their author and often provide his or her email address. Display advertisements are distinguishable from editorial content, and sponsored stories are labeled “advertorial.”

Globegazette.com does not clearly state its ownership by Lee Enterprises, other than in a small copyright notice on the site’s menu sidebar and on the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy pages. “[The website] is a corporate template, so I don't have any comment on that,” David Mayberry, Editor of the Globe Gazette, told NewsGuard in a phone call. “You'll find that at other Lee [Enterprises] papers as well.”

History

The Globe Gazette traces its history to 1858, with the founding of the Cerro Gordo Press, which eventually became the Globe Gazette. The publication was purchased by Lee Enterprises in 1925.
