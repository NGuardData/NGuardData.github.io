Ownership and Financing

The Hartford Courant and Courant.com are owned by Tribune Publishing Company, a publicly traded, Chicago-based media and publishing company formerly known as Tronc Inc. Tribune owns newspapers and publications in major markets, including The Baltimore Sun, New York Daily News, Orlando Sentinel, and its flagship publication, the Chicago Tribune.

Tribune generates its revenue through advertising and subscriptions for its printed and digital products. Its publications have won numerous awards, including a Pulitzer Prize in 2017.

Content

The Courant covers news, politics, and business in Connecticut. Regional coverage includes such topics as the state’s first Farm Aid fundraiser, ecological problems caused by drought, and state and regional political campaigns. The Courant provides extensive coverage of college and professional sports, including Connecticut’s minor league baseball team, The Hartford Yard Goats. The entertainment section includes reviews of local bars and restaurants, and information about upcoming concerts in Connecticut’s major cities. The site and newspaper publish national news and entertainment content from sources including The Associated Press, Tribune News Service, and The Washington Post. The site also publishes an electronic version of the print newspaper online.

The Courant Originals feature showcases original videos about prominent local figures, such as poets, artists, and athletes. Videos also cover regional events and entertainment. The site publishes community content, such as wedding announcements and obituaries, as well as editorials and opinion sections, which are clearly distinguished from news.

The Courant operates a chain of bureaus in smaller cities and publishes a series of local editions, including Hartford Magazine, which provides restaurant reviews, tips for healthy living, and recommendations for traveling in Connecticut.

Credibility

On courant.com, readers can view the publication’s mission statement, which details its goal of delivering timely, relevant information to readers with “honesty and compassion.” Headlines on the site are straightforward, and reflect content. Stories consistently feature on-site reporting and firsthand interviews.

The Courant won a Pulitzer Prize in 1999 for its coverage of a shooting rampage, in which a state lottery worker killed four supervisors and himself. In 2013, The Courant was a Pulitzer Prize finalist in Breaking News Reporting for its in-depth coverage of the massacre at an elementary school in Newtown, Connecticut.

Courant.com identifies a staff member, with contact information, who will respond to and consider reader requests for corrections. A NewsGuard review could not independently find regular corrections on the site. The Courant did not respond to NewsGuard’s email and phone call seeking examples of recently corrected stories.

In 2009, The Courant’s president and publisher acknowledged in a letter to readers that an internal review found that the newspaper had plagiarized the work of some of its competitors, and apologized. Smaller newspapers in Connecticut said that The Courant had repeatedly used their reporting with little or no attribution.

Transparency

The Courant names the authors of its stories and provides information about them. The Contact Us section provides a comprehensive directory of editorial and senior staff, with email addresses. The site’s About page clearly discloses its ownership. Advertising and sponsored articles are clearly labeled.

History

The Courant is the oldest continuously published newspaper in the U.S. A printer in New Haven published the first, four-page weekly edition of The Connecticut Courant in 1764.
