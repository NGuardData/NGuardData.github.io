Ownership and Financing

The Human Rights Campaign, Inc. is a non-profit organization. Its president is political strategist Chad Griffin. According to tax filings, HRC’s total revenue for 2017 was almost $42 million, over three-quarters of which came from donations and grants. HRC, which began as a political action committee (PAC), is affiliated with the PAC Human Rights Campaign Equality Votes. The PAC spends money to boost Democratic Party candidates, according to Opensecrets.org, a campaign finance website maintained by the Center for Responsive Politics. Hrc.org invites readers to donate via the website and has corporate sponsors.

Content

Hrc.org content provides updates on HRC’s campaign for LGBTQ rights, writing on its own activities and policy developments at the state and national levels. It endorses candidates for office and outlines HRC’s position on key political issues. It publishes a daily email on LGBTQ issues. The site hosts a “buyers guide” and annual “Corporate Equality Index” report, both of which rate businesses and large corporations on equality practices in their workplaces.

Credibility

While articles listed as “news” are often opinionated and headlines can contain exaggeration, they fit Hrc.org’s clearly stated mission of advancing LGBTQ rights. Articles are factual and appropriately sourced, although attribution of images is inconsistent. The site does not have a stated corrections policy and does not appear to have published recent corrections. In response to a NewsGuard email, a representative of HRC stated that corrections can be submitted through the general contact form.

The Human Rights Campaign is frequently cited by other news outlets. In 2017, for example, New York Times columnist Frank Bruni relied on HRC data for a feature story on the “worst (and best) places to be gay in America.”

An internal 2015 report obtained by BuzzFeed News, described the organization as an “exclusionary,” “sexist,” and “homogenous”  “white men’s club,” despite campaigning for diversity in the workplace. Griffin responded at the time, "As we fully anticipated, the report flagged problem areas that the organization has already begun to tackle aggressively. We'll continue to address them, one by one, as any serious organization recognizing these challenges would."

Transparency

The site offers detailed information on how it is organized and on its leadership. Articles generally name the author and link to information about content creators. Some articles have generic or unclear references to authors such as “HRC Staff” or “Guest contributor.” A Contact page offers general email addresses for HRC departments.

History

HRC dates to 1980 when Steve Endean, a gay rights activist, founded the Human Rights Campaign Fund as a political action committee working on LGBTQ issues. In 1995, HRC dropped “fund” from its name.
