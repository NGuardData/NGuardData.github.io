viralthread.com  (WEB - en-US)

A website that publishes trending news likely to go viral and shares what it describes as “a bird’s-eye view of what is trending in the media.”

Ownership and Financing

Viral Thread is owned by Jungle Creations, a U.K.-based company that specializes in social media marketing. It operates more than a dozen Facebook pages followed by millions, including the food-focused Twisted and Food Envy pages. 

ViralThread.com runs advertisements.

Content

In its About page, ViralThread.com says its mission is to offer “a bird’s-eye view of what is trending in the media as well as stories about places, people and products you’ve never heard of before.” The site focuses on interesting or outlandish stories that are likely to be popular on social media and go viral, often with headlines meant to entice readers to click through to the story. Examples include “Teenage Waitress Cuts Up Food For Customer And Is Rewarded In The Best Possible Way” and “Vacationing Couple Tragically Die Moments After Taking A Selfie Together.”

The stories are often centered around photos, videos, or posts from social media sites, although Viral Thread sometimes cites reporting and publishes videos from established news sites.

Credibility

Reports on the site are primarily drawn from social media users and other sources, and there is often no way to determine how credible these sources are. Stories will sometimes transcribe interviews from other sites without crediting them. Photos frequently appear with text stories without attribution.

Because of its lack of credit and vetting, NewsGuard has determined that ViralThread.com does not gather and present news responsibly.

Asked about the site’s sourcing standards, Jungle Creations’ head of communications, Hannah Watson, said in an email that the site does not have a “formal crediting system,” but said that one was in the works. Watson added that Viral Thread “is predominantly entertainment news and does not purport to be at the forefront of hard hitting news stories.”

The site does not post a corrections policy. Watson said ViralThread.com publishes corrections when needed and said its staff goes “a long way to ensure all our stories are verified.” She provided an example of a correction posted in April 2018.

Transparency

Readers can click on a writer’s byline to access author pages that list other published work, and most of these pages also provide biographical and contact information. The author page for the site’s content editor, who frequently writes posts, discloses his leadership position.

The site discloses its ownership on its About page.

Advertising is distinguishable from editorial content.

History

The ViralThread.com domain was created in 2014. Jamie Bolding, Jungle Creations’ founder and CEO, created Viral Thread and originally wrote about college life for the site, Watson said.
