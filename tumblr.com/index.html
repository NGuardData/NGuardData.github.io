tumblr.com  (WEB - en-US)

A social network and microblogging platform that is a hub for liberal activism, art, memes, and so-called fandoms.

Ownership and Financing

Tumblr is owned by Oath Inc., a company that was formed when Verizon Communications purchased Yahoo in June 2017. Oath’s other properties include Yahoo!, Yahoo Sports, Yahoo News, Yahoo Mail, HuffPost, TechCrunch, and AOL.

Tumblr makes most of its money from advertising, mostly in the form of sponsored posts that appear on users’ “dashboards.” While Tumblr is profitable, it has repeatedly fallen short of expectations: Its leadership has twice announced goals for the site to produce over $100 million in annual revenue, but has fallen short. The site began experimenting with running advertisements on individual blogs in 2016 but has since abandoned that tack.

Content

Tumblr is a platform, not a news outlet. Content is created by the website’s users. Consequently, the scope of topics covered is extremely broad. However, much of the content on Tumblr is in line with the tastes of its predominantly young, often liberal user base. Examples of popular blogs on the site include The Daily Feminist, This Is Everyday Racism, and Reclaiming The #Latina Tag.

The site is known for its “fandoms,” which are communities of fans typically dedicated to film, book, and television franchises such as Harry Potter, the BBC series Sherlock, and the Cartoon Network show Adventure Time. The site is also popular among artists, for whom it provides a means to promote their work, and social justice-oriented activists.

Tumblr states that it is dedicated to free speech, and the site makes no restrictions on or endorsements of particular political views. Nevertheless, the site is a hub for left-leaning material protesting racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, and other prejudices.

Although Tumblr itself does not produce news, the site is used as a social media platform by many companies and brands, including news outlets. Consequently, some news content on the site comes from reputable outlets including NPR, the Washington Post, and Politico. Political conspiracy theories have not spread on Tumblr with the force they have on some other platforms such as 4chan and Reddit. Conspiracy theories on Tumblr more often take the form of spurious, sometimes joking speculation about the secret lives of celebrities, such as false claims that One Direction member Louis Tomlinson never actually had his baby, that singer Taylor Swift and model Karlie Kloss are dating, and that Demi Lovato has a secret twin sister named Poot.

Tumblr’s most popular content tends to be whimsical, including blogs such as “Lonely Cheetos” (depicting the cheese-flavored snack posed in unusual places;) “Breaded Cats” (photos of cats with their faces stuck through slices of bread;) and “Ugly Renaissance Babies” (a curation of unconventional looking children in Renaissance art.)

Tumblr has attracted attention for the amount of pornography that has been posted in the site. In 2013, TechCrunch reported that approximately 10 percent of the site’s top 200,000 blogs were pornographic. In 2016, research published jointly by Bell Labs and Italian universities IMT Lucca and ISTI-CNR Pisa showed roughly half of Tumblr’s users were either unintentionally exposed to or deliberately consuming pornography.

After its acquisition by Yahoo in 2013, Tumblr established new restrictions on the visibility of pornography on the site, making pornography unsearchable and banning users from directly uploading pornography onto Tumblr. The ubiquity of pornography on Tumblr has led countries including Indonesia and Kazakhstan to block the site. The site is also blocked in China and Iran, although it is not clear whether pornography is the primary reason for the bans.

Credibility

Tumblr does not fact-check posted content and it is unclear whether the company has any mechanisms for removing or limiting the spread of false information. However, the site does have some guidelines relating to credibility. Users are discouraged from posting “deceptive or fraudulent links,” posts must be tagged accurately, and content on the site must be properly attributed to its original source. A link is available for the purpose of reporting misattribution on Tumblr. Users who violate Tumblr’s guidelines can have their accounts suspended.

Content on Tumblr can be shared by “reblogging,” or sharing a post, along with any comments attached to it. Previously, when reblogging, it was possible for users to alter both the original post and its chain of comments. In September 2015, Tumblr changed the way reblogs work to make it impossible to edit another user’s comments.

In February 2018, BuzzFeed News ran a story reporting that “Russian trolls” had run Tumblr accounts garnering hundreds of thousands of interactions while attacking Hillary Clinton in the runup to the 2016 U.S. presidential election. A month later, Tumblr released a list of 84 usernames it believed were connected to “state-sponsored disinformation and propaganda campaigns.” The site removed these accounts.

Tumblr did not respond to two NewsGuard emails asking what procedures, if any, the site has implemented to prevent future efforts at spreading disinformation and propaganda.  

Transparency

Tumblr users are under no obligation to use their real names and many choose not to. Accounts can be created freely and easily, and it is not uncommon for a single user to run multiple Tumblr blogs. However, users are discouraged from mass registering or automating their accounts and from impersonating another person or blog; users caught violating these guidelines can be suspended.

Official sponsored posts provided through Tumblr are labeled as such. However, individual bloggers and users are under no obligation to disclose whether any of the content they post has been sponsored.

Tumblr’s ownership by Oath and Verizon is noted in the website’s Terms of Service. Since 2013, Tumblr has produced two Transparency Reports a year, outlining how many Digital Millennium Copyright Act takedown requests the site received, and how many resulted in content removal. In its report covering July to December 2017, Tumblr reports it received 9,216 DMCA notices, of which it deemed 86.9 percent valid. This resulted in the termination of 2,292 accounts and the removal of 39,303 posts from the site.

History

Tumblr launched in 2007 when its founder, David Karp, was 20 years old. It was run for its first year by Karp and lead developer Marco Arment, and the company only began to staff up when Tumblr already had over one million users. Ads began appearing on Tumblr in May 2012, with a major advertising campaign for Adidas. Karp left Tumblr in November 2017.

The 2012 Obama campaign joined Tumblr in October 2011, marking its emergence as an important political platform. That same year, the Tumblr account “wearethe99percent” popularized the political slogan that came to be associated with the liberal Occupy movement.

Yahoo purchased the site in 2013 for $1.1 billion.
