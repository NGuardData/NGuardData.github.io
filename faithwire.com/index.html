Ownership or Financing

Faithwire was founded by the Christian Broadcasting Network (CBN), a private, for-profit TV network founded in 1960 by televangelist Pat Robertson. The site runs advertisements.

Content

Faithwire’s Managing Editor Dan Andros described the site to the Christian Broadcasting Network as “a faith-based news website targeting millennials.” Coverage focuses on Christian life, practice, and theology, along with conservative political causes. In Summer 2018, Faithwire’s headlines included “How SCOTUS Sending Florist Case Back To State Court Could be a Victory for Religious Liberty,” “Crowdfunded Abortion Doctor Movie Finally Gets Distribution Deal After Hollywood Ignored Filmmakers, Judge Blocked Its Release,” and “School Participates in Repeated Vandalism of Students’ Pro-Life Display.”

Most of Faithwire’s original content is commentary on political and theological matters.

Credibility

Faithwire’s news stories are typically drawn from attributed third-party news organizations. For example, a news article from June 2018 titled “Parents Outraged After VA School District Says It Will No Longer Acknowledge ‘Biological Sex’ in Sex-Ed Program” draws its information from NBC News, Fox 5 DC, among others. Faithwire includes links to its sources. Headlines accurately reflect the content of stories.

Although Faithwire has an Opinion page, opinionated content often appears outside that section. For example, articles headlined “Irish Government Will Force Catholic Hospitals to Perform Abortions Following Devastating Referendum,” “Here Are 10 Good Reasons Why U.S. Cutting Ties With UN Human Rights Council Makes Sense,” and “Creepy Government Video Indoctrinates Children for Pride Month” all ran in the website’s News section.

Because the site regularly runs opinionated articles outside its Opinion section without labeling them, NewsGuard has determined that the site does not responsibly handle the difference between news and information.

Faithwire does not articulate a corrections policy and does not regularly run corrections. The site did not respond to two NewsGuard emails asking about Faithwire’s approach to corrections and about how it handles news and opinion.

Transparency

Articles on the site often include the name of the writer, although some are attributed to “Faithwire Staff.” The site does not provide contact or biographical information about its content creators. Although the site includes a staff page, it only lists a few senior staff members.

Faithwire.com does not disclose its connection to the CBN, including in stories that mention or quote Gordon Robertson, CBN founder Pat Robertson’s son and the CEO of CBN.

Faithwire did not respond to two NewsGuard emails inquiring about the site’s lack of disclosure about its ownership and content creators.

Advertisements are clearly labeled.

History

Faithwire was launched by the Christian Broadcasting Network in 2016.
