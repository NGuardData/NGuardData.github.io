Ownership and Financing

BizPacReview.com does not indicate who owns the website. A Privacy Policy page says the site is owned by BizPac Review Inc., and the site’s About Us page says BizPac Review is based in West Palm Beach, Florida. A March 2018 document filed with the Florida secretary of state lists John R. Smith as BizPac Review, Inc.’s registered agent.

The website runs display advertisements. An Advertise page states BizPac Review also offers petition campaigns, video ads, and sponsored content.

Content

BizPac Review publishes conservative commentary on U.S. political news. For example, BizPac Review often writes about pundit Sean Hannity’s program on Fox News Channel and embeds video clips of the show. Conversely, it will frequently criticize liberals and segments on CNN and MSNBC shows.

The site has pages devoted to news concerning conservative public figures, such as President Donald Trump, pundit Ann Coulter, Fox News Channel host Laura Ingraham, and White House counselor Kellyanne Conway.

BizPac Review republishes articles from the politically conservative Daily Caller News Foundation.

Credibility

BizPac Review rarely conducts its own interviews and reporting. Instead, it bases its commentary on stories reported by other organizations, including Fox, CNN, MSNBC, and The New York Times, as well as social media posts.

BizPac Review’s About Us page notes its political perspective, saying the website “provides breaking news and analysis unfiltered by the liberal bias that has eroded the media’s credibility.” A typical post in January 2019 stated, “Leftist social media users melted down in real time Tuesday when the Associated Press made the allegedly grievous mistake of trying to fact-check members of the Democrat Party.” 

The site generally avoids distorting facts in its commentary and discloses a perspective, which is why NewsGuard has determined that it handles the difference between news and opinion responsibly.

In October 2016, BizPac Review published an unsubstantiated post citing information from TruePundit.com, a conservative website that frequently publishes unsubstantiated conspiracy theories, headlined “Hillary reportedly asked, ‘Can’t we just drone this guy?’ Wikileaks tweets about Assange’s concerns.” The TruePundit.com report cited “State Department sources” and said former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton asked “Can’t we just drone this guy?” of WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange at a 2010 meeting. One day after the BizPacReview.com post was published, Clinton said she did not remember saying this and added that if she did, it would have been a joke. The BizPac Review post was not updated to reflect her statement, and was never corrected.

NewsGuard did not find similar examples of unsubstantiated content on the site, which is why it has determined that BizPac Review meets its standard for responsibly gathering and presenting information.

Although the website does not have a stated corrections policy, NewsGuard located recent examples of corrections published in the form of editor’s notes above stories, explaining previous errors.

Headlines are opinionated and sensational, but generally reflect the content in articles.

Transparency

BizPacReview.com does not disclose its ownership. An archived version of its About Us page from April 2018 said it is a “privately held, for-profit news and opinion website covering news that matters to conservatives throughout Florida and the United States.” The About Us page was not accessible on the site in 2019.

Some ads are labeled or otherwise differentiated from BizPac Review’s editorial content. However, because other ads that look like news stories are not labeled, NewsGuard has determined the site does not meet its standard for clearly labeling advertising.

BizPac Review did not respond to two emails and a phone message inquiring about its unlabeled advertising and its lack of an ownership disclosure.

A BizPac Review Writers page provides short biographical blurbs for members of the site’s staff, some of whom list email or social media contact information. The page names BizPac Review’s CEO and senior editor.

History

BizPac Review’s About Us page says the site was founded in 2009, but a media kit on the website says it was founded in 2011 “to provide a conservative view in Palm Beach County to the notoriously leftist Palm Beach Post.”
