Ownership and Financing

Yellowhammer News is owned by entrepreneur Allison Ross and Tim Howe, a public relations professional, lobbyist, and political campaign consultant, through Yellowhammer Multimedia, LLC, a private company registered in Birmingham, Alabama.

Ross also serves as Yellowhammer News’ publisher and Howe is its editor. Brian Ellis, a former college basketball coach, is Yellowhammer Multimedia’s CEO and registered agent.

Howe, who also writes for the site, was formerly the director of the Alabama Republican Party.

The website was founded in 2012 by Alabama resident Cliff Sims, who worked as an aide to Donald Trump’s 2016 presidential campaign and later held communications positions in the White House. Sims left the White House in 2018 and wrote a tell-all book, “Team of Vipers,” that was released in 2019.

Yellowhammer Multimedia earns revenue through an online apparel store, through advertisements and sponsored content that run on YellowhammerNews.com, and through two annual events which honor influential leaders in Alabama.

Content

YellowhammerNews.com primarily covers Alabama politics and government from a conservative perspective. The site places a particular emphasis on state-level news, such as gun-related bills and oversight of congressional appropriations. It also publishes stories on Alabama sports, including college football and NASCAR, and business in the state.

The site features positive articles about Republican politicians in Alabama, as well as the Trump administration. Typical stories include “Obama era environmental regulations strike again, force closure of APCO’s Gorgas Steam Plant,” and “‘Where Is Leigh Corfman?’: Roy Moore says accuser avoiding giving ‘her testimony under oath.’’’ The latter refers to one of the women who accused the former Republican Senate candidate of sexual misconduct.

The site also publishes articles from The Associated Press and The Daily Caller News Foundation. High-profile opinion contributors have included Ben Shapiro, editor-in-chief of conservative news site The Daily Wire, and Republican Alabama State Senator Del Marsh.

Yellowhammer News also offers free one- and two-minute news broadcasts to Alabama radio stations. These broadcasts are not available on the site, aside from two samples.

Credibility

Yellowhammer News articles are fact-based, citing firsthand interviews, official documents, and other credible sources. Headlines and images accurately reflect content. However, images are not typically attributed to sources.

The website’s story selection promotes a conservative perspective that it does not disclose.For example, February 2019 articles focus on criticism of the state’s Democratic U.S. senator, Doug Jones, from a Republican who said he would seek his seat, such as “Bradley Byrne glad President Trump has entered abortion fight, criticizes Doug Jones for his silence,” and “Byrne: Doug Jones ‘doing whatever Chuck Schumer wants him to do’.” The articles do not include or show an attempt to obtain a response from Jones.

NewsGuard has determined that because Yellowhammer News covers news from a conservative perspective without disclosing this viewpoint, the site does not handle the difference between news and opinion responsibly.

Yellowhammer News updates articles and publishes corrections, typically at the bottom of the story in question.

NewsGuard made two attempts to reach YellowHammer News for comment by email regarding the website’s disclosure of its conservative perspective, but did not receive a response.

Transparency

Articles on YellowhammerNews.com name the author and link to an archive of the writer’s work. Biographies for the site’s staff, including editorial leadership, are provided on a Team page and short biographies are included at the end of some articles. The site also lists a general editorial email address.

Yellowhammer News’ ownership by Tim Howe and Allison Ross, through Yellowhammer Multimedia, is disclosed on the site’s Team page.

The former owner, Sims, took a leave of absence from Yellowhammer News while working for Trump’s presidential campaign in 2016, and sold the site in 2017 before taking a job with Trump’s administration. Stories about him generally refer to his role with Yellowhammer.

Advertisements and sponsored content are clearly distinguished from editorial content.

History

Sims founded Yellowhammer News in 2012, saying he aimed to reach Alabama political and business leaders. In 2017 and 2018, he served as special assistant to the president in the Trump administration, and director of White House message strategy.

Joshua Jones, CEO of Birmingham-based data consulting company StrategyWise, bought Yellowhammer Multimedia from Sims in May 2017. Jones sold his stake to Ross, one of the current owners, in September of that year. Howe was listed as a co-owner in August 2018.

In May 2016, Howe testified as a prosecution witness during the trial of former Alabama House Speaker Mike Hubbard, who was tried for felony ethics violations. Howe testified that Hubbard had received a 5 percent cut of payments that were routed to the Alabama Republican Party through a company he owned, but that he did not know why the payments were set up this way.

Hubbard was convicted in June 2016 on 12 out of 23 felony ethics violations for having used his public position to gain personal profit. A state appeals court in 2018 rejected his appeal.
