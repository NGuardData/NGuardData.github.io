americanlookout.com  (WEB - en-US)

A conservative site that frequently blurs the line between news and opinion and sometimes promotes debunked conspiracy theories.

Proceed with caution: This website severely violates basic standards of credibility and transparency.

Ownership and Financing

American Lookout’s About page states that is led by editor Mike LaChance, a full-time blogger who has previously written for conservative sites Breitbart and Gateway Pundit. American Lookout does not make it clear whether LaChance is American Lookout’s owner. The site runs advertisements.

Content

American Lookout describes itself as “Americans writing for Americans about the news you won’t see anywhere else.” The site displays a conservative viewpoint, frequently running stories critical of Democrats or flattering towards Republicans. Articles on americanlookout.com typically comprise of a short introductory paragraph written by an American Lookout writer, followed by a longer section of text taken from another news source.

Typical headlines have included “Daughter Of Maxine Waters To Collect Over $200K For Working On Her Mother’s 2018 Campaign;” “Media And Democrats Will Grovel Before Stephen Colbert Throughout 2020 Primary;” and “Lindsey Graham: There’s Nothing Democrats Won’t Do To Win (VIDEO).” The site does not have specific content categories or a dedicated opinion section.

Credibility

American Lookout typically credits the news sources from which it draws content. Although some of these — such as CBS News and Fortune magazine — are generally considered reliable, the website has also relied on content from sources that have been found to publish false content, including InfoWars, Gateway Pundit, and RT, a Russian state-owned propaganda outlet.

American Lookout has re-published false or misleading information. For example, a September 2016 article, headlined “Due to Mass Muslim Immigration Sweden Now Has 55 ‘No-Go Zones’ Across The Country,” is based on an RT article that suggested that due to a rise in the Muslim population, Swedish police no longer will patrol certain areas. In fact, according to numerous press accounts, Swedish police routinely operate in the affected areas, and officials have not connected a spike in crime to an influx of Muslim immigrants. In addition, American Lookout illustrated its article with a picture from the U.K., not Sweden.

American Lookout sometimes promotes conspiracist stories. For example, a November 2016 article, headlined “Media Ignored THREE SUSPICIOUS DEATHS Of Men Tied To Hillary – WILL TRUMP INVESTIGATE?,” made the unfounded allegation that Hillary Clinton may be responsible for the deaths of several people, including Democratic staffer Seth Rich. The article states that “During Hillary’s run, three men died in suspicious ways… Exactly when investigations would have damaged the Hillary campaign.” There is no evidence actually linking Clinton to any of the deaths, and in some cases, it is not even clear how their deaths benefitted her.  

Another article, “SHARIA LAW IN LONDON: Muslim Mayor Bans ‘Sexy’ Advertisements” (August 2017), promoted the claim that London Mayor Sadiq Khan is introducing Sharia law in the city because of a push to tone down advertising in the public transit system. The site wrote: “This is how Sharia Law supplants Western values and culture. It doesn’t happen overnight, it creeps in gradually, step by step. In London, Muslim mayor Sadiq Khan just made a decision which would be hailed in Saudi Arabia.” The ban under discussion targeted “unrealistic expectations about body image and health,” according to The Guardian, and affected only advertising on the city’s transportation network. There is no evidence that it is part of an effort to bring Islamic law to London. Moreover, Khan pushed to legalize gay marriage in Britain in 2013 and has supported the opening of more pubs in London — neither of them positions that suggest a fundamentalist approach.

Similarly, a March 2017 article, “FLASHBACK: How And WHY Globalist Billionaire George Soros Created The Muslim Refugee Crisis,” made the unfounded claim that Soros, a billionaire and liberal philanthropist, “is intent on destroying national borders and creating a global governance structure with unlimited powers” and speculates that his “bearish derivative positions” are a ploy to profit off migrant-caused chaos in Europe and the U.S. The article failed to explain how Soros created the refugee crisis.

Because of its repeated promotion of conspiracy theories, its reliance on dubious news sources, and its apparent failure to fact check its sources, NewsGuard has determined that American Lookout fails to responsibly gather and present information and has repeatedly publishedfalse information.

American Lookout presents itself as a news site, does not feature an opinion section, and does not disclose a particular ideological agenda. Nevertheless, the site consistently runs content strongly favoring U.S. President Donald Trump and attacking Democrats. Examples of opinionated articles have included “IDIOT: Joy Behar Says Republicans Won The Senate Because Of Gerrymandering (VIDEO)” (November 2018); “SHAMELESS: Obama Claims We’ve Never Had Politicians Just Making Stuff Up (VIDEO)” (October 2018); and “JOBS NOT MOBS: Trump Rolls Out Brilliant New Slogan At Montana Rally (VIDEO)” (October 2018).

Because it portrays itself as a news source while advancing an undisclosed viewpoint, NewsGuard has determined that American Lookout does not adequately distinguish news from opinion.

American Lookout does not appear to run corrections or feature a stated corrections policy.

The site did not respond to two emails seeking comment on its promotion of conspiracy theories, use of questionable news sources, handling of the difference between news and opinion, and approach to corrections.

Transparency

Americanlookout.com features an About Us page that notes, “We’re led by Mike LaChance,” and describes his professional experience. LaChance is identified as the author for most posts on the site.

LaChance is identified as the leader of the site, but it is not explicitly disclosed whether he is AmericanLookout.com’s owner.

Although display advertisements on the site are distinguished from editorial content, articles often feature sections titled “You Might Like” that mimic editorial content and link to promotional content. These sections are not labeled as promotional. Because content in the “You Might Like” sections is not tagged as advertising or sponsored content, NewsGuard has determined that AmericanLookout.com does not meet the standard for labeling advertising.

The site did not respond to three NewsGuard emails seeking comment on its failure to disclose ownership and its labeling of advertising.

History

The site’s domain was created in May 2016.
