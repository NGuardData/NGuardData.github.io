Ownership and Financing

Patheos is owned by BN Media, a privately held company based in Virginia Beach, Virginia. BN Media also owns Beliefnet, a lifestyle and spirituality site that is not affiliated with a particular religion, and Affinity4, a company that helps raise funds for nonprofit organizations.

Patheos.com earns revenue through advertisements.

Content

Patheos is a collection of columns and posts by religious leaders, practitioners, and commentators from a wide variety of religions, including different sects of Christianity, Islam, Buddhism, Judaism, and Hinduism, as well as Atheism and Paganism. The site’s About Us page describes Patheos as “the premier online destination to engage in the global dialogue about religion and spirituality, and to explore and experience the world's beliefs.” The site says it offers “credible and balanced information about religion.”

Contributors include Deacon Greg Kandra, a CBS News producer-turned-Catholic deacon; Hemant Mehta, who has written extensively about Atheism; and Scot McKnight, an evangelical New Testament scholar. Contributors, or bloggers, have their own pages, which are housed within larger categories on the site. For example, McKnight’s blog is called Jesus Creed, and is housed within the Evangelical section.

Blogs generally apply a religious perspective to matters such as family, relationships, and life events. The site also delves into political issues, providing both liberal and conservative viewpoints, with content categorized as Politics Blue or Politics Red.

The site houses a Religion Library, containing factual information about the core beliefs and histories of the world’s major religions and several smaller ones, including Scientology and Taoism. The Library has a feature allowing users to compare religions across categories, including origins, history, beliefs, and ethics.

Credibility

Articles on the site are generally well-sourced and fact-based, including first-hand interviews and content from credible news outlets such as The Guardian, the Washington Post, and The Daily Beast. Religious postings often base commentary on seminal texts, such as the Bible, the Quran, the Talmud, and the Pali Canon. Headlines accurately reflect content.

Content sometimes includes the personal religious views of the writer, but opinionated content is generally run in the Blogs section or under politics, packaged as having a perspective — Politics Blue for liberal/Democratic, Politics Red for conservative/Republican.

Blogs are generally marked with the author’s religious affiliation, and link to a biography.  Each blog has an About section explaining the religious or political beliefs and background of the author. For example, the About section of a blog page called The Mormon Politico states that the blog “approaches politics and world affairs from the perspective of a lifelong Mormon who identifies as a left-leaning moderate.” The author of the blog, Gary Ashcroft, is described as “an attorney who works on national security issues for a centrist Democratic think tank.”

The site does not post a corrections policy, but corrections are regularly published, usually marked as an “update.”

Transparency

Articles are credited to authors, with biographical information, although no contact information is provided. The site does not disclose its ownership or editorial leaders in a user-friendly manner. The site’s co-founders, Leo and Cathie Brunnick, are identified as co-founders in searchable biographies, but not in a way that a user would easily find.

Advertisements are clearly distinguished from editorial content.  

Patheos did not respond to three messages sent by NewsGuard through its online contact portal and two emails seeking comment on its disclosure of the site’s ownership and management.

History

Patheos was founded in 2008. In September 2016, the site was acquired by BN Media as a subsidiary of Beliefnet, Inc.
