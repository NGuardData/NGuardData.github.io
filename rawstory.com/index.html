Ownership and Financing

John Byrne, the site’s founder, is the chairman, CEO, and controlling partner of Raw Story Media, Inc., a privately held company registered in Massachusetts that owns the site. Byrne is also controlling partner of AlterNet Media, which publishes another left-leaning news site, and is the founder and executive director of a Miami nonprofit that helps expand access to HIV prevention services.

Raw Story earns revenue through advertisements and sponsored content. 

Content

Raw Story publishes articles on national, international, technology, and media news, and has a section focused on U.S. President Donald Trump. Much of Raw Story’s daily reporting is devoted to short blurbs on the latest news, often with a headline and tone critical of conservatives and Republican politicians.

The site also publishes original, long-form journalism. A 2018 story reported on how the city of Seattle handles its homeless population, for example. A 2016 investigation detailed healthcare company Mylan’s aggressive marketing of an allergy drug before it dramatically raised the price.

Roxanne Cooper, editor and publisher of Raw Story and AlterNet, told NewsGuard that Raw Story employs 15 full-time staff members and dozens of freelancers to produce original content. It also publishes syndicated content from major media outlets including Reuters, Agence France-Presse, Salon, and ProPublica.

Credibility

The site’s original journalism is well-sourced, routinely citing multiple experts and reputable studies. In 2008, Raw Story was a finalist for an Online Journalism Award for small newsrooms, for a multi-part series on former Democratic Alabama Governor Don Siegelman, who was convicted of bribery.

The shorter breaking stories published on Raw Story are often one-sided and generally rely on social media, online videos, and reporting conducted by other news outlets. In 2015, The site published a false report about Costco removing a dinosaur cake from its bakery after a customer complained that it was Satanic. After a reader alerted the site that the story wasn’t true, a new story was posted acknowledging that it was hoax.  

Cooper told NewsGuard that all original content goes through “the same thorough fact-checking process that would be conducted at any legitimate news organization.” Byrne, the site’s founder, sent NewsGuard a copy of Raw Story’s reporting guidelines, which say that “Raw Story is committed to publishing fair and accurate content.”

Cooper said opinionated content appears in a section labeled “opinion.” However, a review of the site found a number of opinion articles, with a “commentary” label accessible only after clicking on the story, among the site’s news coverage. An October 2018 article, for example, was titled “Our republic is in crisis — and it’s killing us. Can we really claim we didn’t see it coming?” Another article was headlined “Here’s how Trump supporters get convinced that patently untrue ‘false flag’ conspiracy theories are real.”

Because Raw Story is not clear about its liberal perspective and mixes news and opinion,  NewsGuard has determined that it does not handle the difference between news and opinion responsibly.

Instructions on how to report errors are included at the end of each article and at the bottom of every page. The site regularly updates articles, noting what was corrected either in the story itself or as part of a separate post.

Transparency

Articles on Rawstory.com have bylines that link to an archive of the writer’s work. A staff directory, linked at the bottom of each page, lists names and email addresses of editors and reporters. Almost all staff members provide their biographical information and some include links to Twitter profiles. A mailing address is provided, as is Cooper’s phone number.

Raw Story’s ownership by Raw Story Media, Inc. is disclosed in a copyright mark at the bottom of each page and as part of the site’s staff directory, which lists the company’s leadership.

Advertisements and sponsored content are distinguished from editorial content.

History

Raw Story launched in 2004, a year after Byrne graduated from Oberlin College. It was positioned as a progressive alternative to The Drudge Report, a right-leaning news site.
