elitedaily.com  (WEB - en-US)

An online publication launched in 2012 targeting millennial women. Elite Daily also publishes content across numerous social media platforms under the tagline “The Voice of Generation-Y.”

Ownership and Financing

Elitedaily.com is owned by Bustle Digital Group, a private media and publishing company that also owns Bustle, Romper, and The Zoe Report. The site runs advertisements.

Content

Elitedaily.com publishes daily articles focused primarily on issues relating to millennial women. Articles cover news, lifestyle and fashion, celebrity news and entertainment, fitness and health, relationships and intimacy, and travel. Many articles are published as advice pieces or lists, such as “5 Hot Pictures To Send Your Dating App Match That They Won’t Be Able To Resist” and “13 Experiences To Buy In Your Twenties Instead Of An Expensive Couch.”

Elitedaily.com also publishes stories related to politics and social issues, such as “Can The 2018 Elections Be Hacked? Experts Think So, & Here We Go Again,” as well as more humorous pieces, like “12 Of The Funniest Memes About A Possible Trump Impeachment To Keep You Going.”

Credibility

Under previous owners, Elitedaily.com was accused by other news sites of airing misogynistic and sexist perspectives. In 2013, Jezebel.com, for example, criticized Elitedaily.com for an article titled “How to turn a ho into a housewife” and for writing that “a woman’s value depreciates over time.”

Since Bustle Digital Group purchased elitedaily.com in April 2017, the publication has sought to improve its editorial standards, it has said. In January 2018, Bustle Digital Group published an editorial on elitedaily.com titled “Bustle Digital Group News Policy and Ethics.” The editorial said the company was dedicated to following ethical journalism practices, including fact-checking, fairness and accuracy in reporting, and reliance of named, credible sources.

Elitedaily.com typically cites multiple sources in its articles and links to other outlets. For example, a September 2018 story headlined “How To Get Better at Multitasking,” quotes numerous experts, including life coaches and medical professionals knowledgeable about the human brain.

Headlines accurately reflect the content of stories.

The site regularly runs corrections, marked as such at the top of articles that have been corrected.

Transparency

Articles include bylines that link to the author’s biographical and contact information. A Contact Us page can be used for general comments and advertising inquiries, and the About Us page identifies company leadership and links to the corporate Bustle Digital Group website.

Elitedaily.com apparently does not always distinguish sponsored content from editorial offerings. For example, a May 2018 article titled “JetBlue's SPF Sale Can Get You 30 Percent Off Flights To Bermuda, So Get Packing” promoted a JetBlue sale, but did not indicate whether the post was sponsored by JetBlue or whether Elitedaily.com gets a commission on sales generated by the article. Another unlabeled article, titled “These Millennial Lilac Travel Accessories Will Take Your Wanderlust Global,” featured a list of items that can be purchased through embedded links. These articles resemble articles that appears on other sites that are paid placements meant to generate sales.

The site did not respond to two emails from NewsGuard seeking more information on these and similar articles on the site that were not labeled as advertising.

History

Elite Daily was launched in 2012 as an online publication, elitedaily.com, by three undergrads at Pace University in New York.  In 2015, the founders sold the startup to the Daily Mail, which sold it to Bustle in 2017.
