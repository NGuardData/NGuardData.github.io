Ownership and Financing

The Bismarck Tribune is owned by Lee Enterprises, a publicly traded, Davenport, Iowa-based company that owns more than 40 daily newspapers, including The St. Louis Post-Dispatch.

Lee Enterprises’ newspapers generate revenue from advertising and subscriptions to their printed and digital editions. Online users have to purchase digital subscriptions for unlimited access to content.

Content

The Bismarck Tribune publishes local and statewide news on subjects ranging from politics and government to sports. Typical stories include coverage of a gas leak at a refinery, a preview of an annual Santa-themed organized run, and a report about budget requests made by North Dakota’s judicial branch.

The site also contains a classifieds section and a community section, which posts information on local events. The website publishes news stories from wire services including The Associated Press and Forum News Service, a North Dakota-based service. Bismarcktribune.com also publishes electronic editions of the print newspaper.

Credibility

The news published on bismarcktribune.com generally relies on original reporting, basing information on on-the-scene reporting and statements of public officials and local residents.

In 2018, the newspaper won approximately a dozen awards for reporting and photography from the North Dakota Newspaper Association, including a first-place award for reporting on an officer-involved shooting.

The website posts editorials, letters to the editors and columnists in a separate Opinion section. While some editorials and columns are not labeled at the top of the story, the content is all posted in the same section on the website, and links that come up in online searches identify the content as opinion in the headline.

The Bismarck Tribune publishes corrections in italics at the top or bottom of a story.Headlines are typically fact-based, accurate, and reflect the content of the stories.

Transparency

The website’s Contact page lists a phone number for the newsroom, as well as forms through which readers can submit news tips or letters to the editor. It also links to a directory for the staff page, which lists names, titles, and phone numbers for its employees.

Stories often name their author and link to a page with an archive of the author’s work. Contact information for writers is also often included at the bottom of stories. Some stories only name “Bismarck Tribune staff” as the author, but this tends to only occur on short articles previewing upcoming events.

The website does not disclose its ownership by Lee Enterprises on the site in a user-friendly manner. Steve Wallick, the newspaper’s editor, told NewsGuard, “The information that the Bismarck Tribune is owned by Lee Enterprises appears on Page A1 daily. So it appears in our e-edition online.”

Advertising is distinguishable from editorial content.

History

The paper was founded in 1873. The Bismarck Tribune won a Pulitzer Prize in 1938 for its reporting on extreme drought in North Dakota.
