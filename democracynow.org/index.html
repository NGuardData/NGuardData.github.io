democracynow.org  (WEB - en-US)

The website for the liberal news program Democracy Now, carried on publicly-supported and community radio and television outlets, which describes itself as a “Daily Grassroots Global News Hour.” The program is produced by liberal journalist and show co-host Amy Goodman.

Ownership and Financing

Democracy Now is produced by Democracy Now Productions Inc., a non-profit 501(c)(3) organization. The program was co-founded by journalists, including current co-hosts Amy Goodman and Juan González, with the stated goal of creating an independent, alternative news source. Democracy Now is funded by contributions and says it does not accept financing from advertising, underwriting, or government. The website features multiple links soliciting donations to Democracy Now.

Content

Democracy Now is a radio, web, and television program. All three air simultaneously and the website hosts the content from all broadcasts. Video segments on democracynow.org are often accompanied by transcripts as well as text describing the content. One section includes content that is exclusive to the site.

Democracy Now is avowedly liberal. The website’s Staff page states that the outlet’s team includes “some of this country's leading progressive journalists.” Articles in August 2018 included “ICE Arrests Husband Taking Pregnant Wife to Hospital to Give Birth, Forcing Her to Drive Alone;” “Angela Davis: Aretha Franklin Offered to Post Bail for Me, Saying ‘Black People Will Be Free’;” and “Military Cover-Up? 100s of Migrants Feared Dead in Mass Grave at AZ’s Barry Goldwater Bombing Range.”

The organization’s programs are broadcast by some National Public Radio and other public radio stations, including community, college, and satellite stations. Its TV broadcasts are carried by some Public Broadcast Service outlets and community stations. A portion of the programs are broadcast in Spanish on radio stations in the U.S., Central and South America, and Europe.

Credibility

News and commentary on the site mostly comes from a liberal perspective, as the content largely consists of segments in which the hosts, Goodman and González, interview guests who are involved in liberal causes, from abortion rights to opposing the Trump Administration’s border policies. Videos often feature interviews with activists and reporters on the ground who appear on-screen speaking directly with the Democracy Now hosts,

Information drawn from other media outlets is usually credited to them, with links. Content on the site’s Headlines section -- a digest of the rest of the site’s content -- does not link to outside sources, although many of the summaries name the original source and the stories are attributed elsewhere on the site. The website has a dedicated Columns section for outright advocacy and opinion writing.

The website does not regularly publish corrections, have a dedicated corrections page, or feature a stated corrections policy. Democracy Now did not respond to two NewsGuard emails asking about the site’s corrections procedures.

Democracy Now has won multiple awards for its work, including the United Nations Correspondents Association Global Prize Gold Medal in 2016 for reporting on the Dakota Access Pipeline protests. During the protests, Goodman was charged with engaging in a riot, but a judge threw out the case for lack of evidence.

In 2008, Goodman became the first journalist to win the Right Livelihood Award -- awarded by a liberal, pro-conservation group -- for, in the words of the prize committee, “developing an innovative model of truly independent political journalism.”

In 1998, Democracy Now aired a documentary, “Drilling and Killing: Chevron and Nigeria’s Oil Dictatorship,” which reported on the Chevron Corporation’s role in the killing of two Nigerian activists. The documentary won the 1998 George Polk Award for Radio Reporting.

Transparency

Most articles on Democracy Now do not name their authors. The site features a Staff page naming the site’s editorial staff and leaders, but there is no way to determine which writer produced which story. The site does not disclose the identities of Democracy Now’s donors.

Democracy Now did not respond to two NewsGuard emails asking about its approach to disclosing information about content creators.

History

Democracy Now was founded in 1996 as a public radio program. The program initially ran on the liberal Pacifica Radio network. It added simultaneous television and online broadcasts in 2001 and separated from Pacifica in 2002.
