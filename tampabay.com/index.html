Ownership and Financing

The Tampa Bay Times, formerly known as the St. Petersburg Times, is the flagship newspaper of the Times Publishing Company, which is owned by the Poynter Institute for Media Studies, a nonprofit journalism training and research organization in St. Petersburg, Florida.

The site runs advertisements and sponsored content.

Content

Tampabay.com publishes news articles and investigative pieces from the Tampa Bay area and greater Florida. Local and state news is largely gathered through original reporting and interviews. The site also publishes national news from the Associated Press.

The site divides its content into News (including Breaking News, Business & Finance, Education, and Health), Politics, Sports, and Opinion. The Politics section includes “The Buzz,” a blog about Florida politics, and a link to PolitiFact, the national political fact-checking organization. Opinion includes Letters to the Editor and pieces from the paper’s six columnists. A Special Topics page features articles on a variety of subjects,ranging from an investigative report on Scientology that won a Pulitzer Prize in 2010, to local craft beer. The Things to Do section highlights cultural events, restaurants, and shopping in the Tampa Bay area.

Credibility

The Tampa Bay Times has won 12 Pulitzer Prizes, most recently in 2016 for Local Reporting and for Investigative Reporting.

In 2007, The Tampa Bay Times, then called The St. Petersburg Times, launched PolitiFact, a Pulitzer Prize-winning not-for-profit national news organization that focuses on fact-checking. PolitiFact won a Pulitzer Prize in 2009 for National Reporting. In 2018, direct ownership of PolitiFact transferred from the newspaper to the Poynter Institute for Media Studies.

The newspaper regularly endorses candidates in local and national political races. Since 1948, it has endorsed Democratic candidates in the general presidential election. These editorial endorsements are separate from its news section.The Tampa Bay Times also makes endorsements in primaries for national and local offices.

The site regularly publishes corrections. A “Standard of Accuracy” states that it aims to “get it right the first time,” and offers a number of ways to contact the editorial team if readers want to recommend corrections.

Transparency

All articles clearly name the writer, linking to background and contact information. The Contact Us page lists the names and contact information for different departments, as well as editorial and leadership staff.

Advertisements and sponsored content are clearly labeled. Images are clearly attributed.

History

The Tampa Bay Times dates to 1884, when the first issues of a weekly Florida paper, the West Hillsborough Times, were published. The paper moved to St. Petersburg in 1892, and was bought in 1912 by Indiana publisher Paul Poynter. By the 1920s, the Times began publishing seven days a week. Poynter’s son, Nelson, ran the paper for four decades. When he died in 1978, he willed most of his stock to what is now the Poynter Institute, a private, nonprofit journalism training and research organization.

Tampabay.com was registered in 1995. In 2012, the paper changed its name from the St. Petersburg Times to the Tampa Bay Times. In 2016, it acquired the more conservative Tampa Tribune.
