informationliberation.com  (WEB - en-US)

A website publishing political news and conservative commentary that exaggerates facts to advance its opinions.

Proceed with caution: This website severely violates basic standards of credibility and transparency.

Ownership and Financing

Chris Menahan is the Maryland-based publisher of Information Liberation, according to the site’s Twitter account. The site provides no details about Menahan, and little information about him exists elsewhere online.

Information Liberation earns revenue from advertising.

Content

Information Liberation publishes political news and analysis that is favorable to conservatives and right-leaning causes. Stories generally consist of a collection of tweets, video clips, and quotes from other news outlets, with a few sentences of the author’s original commentary. Menahan appears to produce all of the stories. Typical stories in 2018 praised President Donald Trump and Justice Brett Kavanaugh, while criticizing their opponents. The perceived censorship of conservative news outlets by large tech companies, and commentary by late-night comedians and celebrities about politics are also popular topics. The sites houses videos separately and sorts its stories according to how many reader comments they generate.

Credibility

Information Liberation does not appear to conduct original reporting, but articles regularly source information from a range of established media outlets such as Fox News, The Associated Press, and CNN. The site also frequently cites tweets as sources of information.

Information Liberation does not disclose a political ideology, describing itself as “a news site dedicated to pursuing the truth wherever it leads, without apology and without care for being politically correct.” Coverage, however, exclusively advances conservative narratives.

Stories target opponents of President Trump, sometimes by distorting facts. For example, a 2018 story led with the headline, “TREASON: Rod Rosenstein 'Plotted to Secretly Record Trump In The WH' to Get Him Ousted Under 25th Amendment,” Rosenstein was not charged with treason. The article also said the FBI “committed treason and plotted to overthrow the President of the United States,” and was backed by The New York Times. Information Liberation did not provide evidence to support these claims.

Information Liberation also failed to correct false information in a story about the August 2018 shooting at the Madden NFL video game tournament in Jacksonville, Florida. The story stated that the shooter, David Katz, “hated Trump supporters” and had used the name “ravenchamps” to criticize them on the Reddit platform. The Reddit account did not belong to Menahan, which far right sites Infowars and Gateway Pundit noted in later corrections. Information Liberation left its article unchanged. NewsGuard has not found visible corrections on the site.

Headlines on Information Liberation sometimes exaggerate or misrepresent facts. One September 2018 story about Christine Blasey Ford, the woman who accused then-Supreme Court nominee Kavanaugh of sexual assault, stated, “Christine Ford Raises Over $700,000 Through GoFundMe Campaigns,” giving the impression that Ford set up the fundraisers herself, when in reality, the campaigns were started on her behalf. Another September 2018 headline, “Michelle Obama Encourages People Who ‘Know Nothing About Nothing’ To Vote,” misrepresented a quote made by former first lady Michelle Obama during a rally in Las Vegas. The first lady was referring to herself as a teenager when she said she “didn’t know nothing about nothing.”

Because of its injection of political opinion in news stories and distortion of facts without correction, NewsGuard has concluded that Information Liberation does not handle the difference between news and opinion responsibly, or avoid the use of deceptive headlines.

Information Liberation did not respond to NewsGuard’s two emails and Facebook message seeking comment on its editorial practices.

Transparency

Menahan is the only author named on the site, and he is also the publisher of Information Liberation, according to the site’s Twitter account. Informationliberation.com provides no information about Menahan on the About page, the Contact page, or on stories written by Menahan. Readers can contact the site using one email address provided on the Contact page.

Information Liberation did not respond to NewsGuard‘s two emails and Facebook message seeking comment on the site’s lack of disclosure about Menahan and any other editors or content creators.

Advertisements are clearly distinguished from editorial content.

History

According to the About page, the site was founded in 2005.
