Ownership and Financing

BearingArms.com is owned by Salem Media Group, a publicly held company that runs Townhall Media, a network of conservative news sites that includes BearingArms.com. Salem calls itself “America’s leading radio broadcaster, Internet content provider, and magazine and book publisher targeting audiences interested in Christian and family-themed content and conservative values.”

Salem Media Group’s websites, including Bearing Arms, earn revenue through advertising. Other Townhall Media sites include HotAir.com and RedState.com.

Content

An About page on the site says BearingArms.com believes in three principles: “In a pre-existing, inalienable right of self-defense; that this right includes the use of contemporary arms, ammunition, and accouterments; [and] that citizens choosing to exercise their right of arms owe a duty to society to master their arms and use them in a judicious and responsible manner.”

The site’s articles, including those labeled as news, are written from a pro-gun rights perspective. Articles invariably accentuate an aspect of the gun debate that favors the gun rights side, with headlines such as “Yet Another Mass Casualty Event In South Carolina Shows How Wrong Gun Control Is” and “Chelsea Handler Illustrates The Ignorance Of The Anti-Gun Left.”

The website’s gun-related articles often deal with firearm policy and consumer firearm news. Posts often detail situations in which people wielding guns thwarted potential crimes. In 2016, the site launched a campaign to highlight how guns and self-defense can help victims of domestic violence.

Credibility

Most posts on Bearing Arms link to original reporting done elsewhere -- including mainstream news outlets such as The Associated Press and Politico.com and conservative sites such as The Daily Caller and The Washington Free Beacon.

The gun debate in the U.S. often features the different sides citing different statistics to make their points. NewsGuard reviewed BearingArms.com’s use of statistics and found them to accurately reflect data from government and other sources.

An April 2018 post headlined “Gun Crime in London Soars 42 Percent” references a BBC report and includes a link to London Metropolitan Police data that back up the article. Another article, headlined “Despite Increases In Gun Ownership, Accidents Decrease,” relies on data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention indicating that adjusted for population growth, accidental gun deaths have declined 48 percent since 1999.

Headlines on the site are opinionated, but accurately reflect the content of articles. The site runs correction notices, typically at the bottom of articles, indicating how the story has been corrected.

Transparency

BearingArms.com displays a prominent Townhall Media logo at the bottom of each website page. However, NewsGuard could not locate information about its ownership by Salem Media Group, other than in a small-print copyright notice and in legal language in the Terms of Use page.

Townhall Media vice president and general manager Jonathan Garthwaite told NewsGuard in an email that he was not sure why the site does not display Salem Media logo, and said he believes the site should disclose the information more prominently.

The site does not include any contact information, except for a Salem email address on a terms of use page. The site’s writers usually have brief biographies that appear under their articles, but no names or contact information is provided about the site’s management. Garthwaite said the website has been without a “stand-alone editor” since a previous editor’s 2017 death.

Advertising and sponsored content are clearly distinguished from editorial content.

History

BearingArms.com was founded in May 2013.
